Jumping Jacks

Focus Full Body Warm-Up
Type Cardio
Difficulty Easy

How to Perform Jumping Jacks:

  • Starting Position: Stand upright with your feet together. Let your arms rest at your sides.
  • Jumping Motion: Jump while spreading your legs apart, wider than shoulder-width. Simultaneously raise your arms overhead until your hands almost touch.
  • Return to Start: Jump again, bringing your legs back together. Lower your arms to your sides.
  • Repeat: Perform this movement rhythmically for a set time or number of repetitions.

Wall Push-Ups

Focus Upper Body
Type Strength
Difficulty Easy

How to Perform Wall Push Ups:

  • Set Up: Stand facing a wall, about an arm's length away. Place your hands on the wall at shoulder height and slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Fingers should point upward.
  • Body Position: Step your feet back slightly to create a lean, keeping your body in a straight line from head to heels. Engage your core to maintain a neutral spine.
  • Lowering Phase: Bend your elbows and lean toward the wall, keeping them at about a 45-degree angle from your torso. Lower your chest until your nose or chest is close to the wall.
  • Pushing Phase: Push through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position.
  • Repeat: Perform the movement for a set number of repetitions (e.g., 10–15) or time (e.g., 30 seconds).

Bodyweight Squats

Focus Lower Body
Type Strength
Difficulty Easy

How to Perform Bodyweight Squats:

  • Starting Position: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Point your toes slightly outward, ensuring alignment with your knees. Engage your core and keep your chest lifted.
  • Lowering Phase: Push your hips back as if sitting into a chair. Bend your knees and lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground (or as low as your mobility allows). Keep your weight on your heels and knees tracking over your toes. Engage your core to maintain a neutral spine.
  • Rising Phase:Press through your heels and straighten your legs to return to the starting position. Squeeze your glutes at the top without locking your knees. Lower your chest until your nose or chest is close to the wall.
  • Rising Phase: Press through your heels and straighten your legs to return to the starting position. Squeeze your glutes at the top without locking your knees.
  • Repeat: Perform for a set number of repetitions (e.g., 10–20) or time (e.g., 30–60 seconds).

Plank

Focus Core Strength
Type Stability
Difficulty Normal

How to Perform Bodyweight Squats:

  • Starting Position: Begin in a prone position on the floor. Place your hands directly under your shoulders, as if preparing for a push-up, or rest on your forearms with elbows aligned under shoulders. Extend your legs straight back with toes on the floor. Engage your core to keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  • Hold the Position: Keep your head neutral (avoid tilting it up or down). Engage your glutes and legs to maintain stability. Avoid sagging or arching your back; your body should remain straight.
  • Duration:Hold the plank for a set time (e.g., 20–60 seconds or longer, depending on your fitness level).
  • Rest and Repeat: Rest for a short period and repeat for your desired number of sets.
