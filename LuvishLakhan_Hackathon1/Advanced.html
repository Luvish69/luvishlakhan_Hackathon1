One arm Push Up

Focus Upper Body and Core Stability
Type Strength
Difficulty Hard

How to Perform One arm Push Up:

  • Starting Position: Begin in a modified push-up position with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart for balance. Place one hand on the ground directly under your shoulder, fingers pointing forward. Tuck your other arm behind your back or keep it at your side. Engage your core and maintain a straight line from head to heels.
  • Lowering Phase: Slowly bend your supporting arm, lowering your chest toward the ground. Keep your body straight and avoid twisting or tilting. Lower as far as your strength allows while maintaining control.
  • Pushing Phase: Push through your palm to straighten your arm and return to the starting position. Maintain stability and balance throughout the movement.
  • Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions on one arm before switching to the other.

Box Jumps

Focus Lower Body
Type Endurance, Cardio
Difficulty Normal

How to Perform Box Jumps:

  • Set Up: Stand in front of a sturdy box or platform, with your feet about shoulder-width apart. Engage your core and keep your chest lifted. Place the box at a height appropriate for your fitness level (start low if you're a beginner).
  • Preparatory Phase: Bend your knees slightly and push your hips back into a quarter-squat position. Swing your arms backward to generate momentum. Engage your core to maintain a neutral spine.
  • Jump Phase: Explosively push through your heels and extend your legs, swinging your arms forward as you jump. Aim to land softly on the box with both feet, ensuring your knees are slightly bent to absorb the impact.
  • Landing Phase: Land with your feet flat and knees tracking over your toes, not caving inward. Stand up fully on the box to complete the movement.
  • Dismount: Step down one foot at a time (safer for joints), or jump off carefully if you're experienced and confident in your landing technique.
  • Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions or for a set time.

High Knee Sprints

Focus Lower Body
Type Cardio and Endurance
Difficulty Hard

How to Perform High Knee Sprints:

  • Starting Position: Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart. Engage your core, and keep your chest lifted and shoulders relaxed. Place your arms bent at a 90-degree angle, ready to move in rhythm with your legs.
  • High Knee Motion: Begin by running in place or moving forward, lifting one knee at a time toward your chest as high as you can. Alternate legs rapidly, keeping your movements controlled. Pump your arms in sync with your legs, with your opposite arm moving forward with each lifted knee.

Jumping Lunges

Focus Lower Body
Type Strength
Difficulty Hard

How to Perform Jumping Lunges:

  • Starting Position: Begin in a lunge position with your right leg forward, your left leg back, and both knees bent at a 90-degree angle. Keep your chest lifted, core engaged, and hands either on your hips or at your sides for balance.
  • Jump Phase: Explosively jump upward, pushing through both feet. Switch legs mid-air so that you land with your left leg forward and your right leg back in a lunge position.
  • Landing Phase:Land softly on the balls of your feet to reduce impact. Immediately bend both knees into a lunge position, maintaining proper form (front knee aligned over the ankle).
  • Repeat: Continue alternating legs in a rhythmic, controlled manner for the desired number of repetitions or time.
