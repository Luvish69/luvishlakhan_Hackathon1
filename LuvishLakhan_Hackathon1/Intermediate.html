Push Ups

Focus Chest and Triceps
Type Bodyweight Exercise
Difficulty Normal

How to Perform Push Ups:

  • Starting Position: Begin in a high plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core to avoid sagging. Position your feet together or slightly apart for balance.
  • Lowering Phase: Bend your elbows and lower your chest toward the ground. Keep your elbows at about a 45-degree angle to your body. Lower yourself until your chest nearly touches the floor (or as far as your strength allows).
  • Pushing Phase: Press through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position. Keep your core tight and avoid locking your elbows.
  • Repeat:Perform for a set number of repetitions (e.g., 10–20) or time (e.g., 30–60 seconds).

Burpees

Focus Full Body
Type Cardio
Difficulty Normal

How to Perform Burpees:

  • Starting Position: Stand upright with feet shoulder-width apart and arms at your sides.
  • Squat and Lower: Bend your knees and lower into a squat, placing your hands on the ground in front of you.
  • Kick Back: Jump or step your feet back into a high plank position, keeping your body in a straight line.
  • Push-Up (Optional): Perform a push-up by lowering your chest to the ground and pressing back up.
  • Return to Squat: Jump or step your feet back toward your hands to return to the squat position.
  • Explosive Jump: Jump straight up, reaching your arms overhead.
  • Repeat: Land softly and immediately transition into the next repetition.

Pistol Squats

Focus Lower Body
Type Strength
Difficulty Hard

How to Perform Pistol Squats:

  • Starting Position:Stand upright with feet together. Extend one leg straight out in front of you, keeping it off the ground. Stretch your arms out in front of you for balance, or keep them at your sides.
  • Lowering Phase: Shift your weight onto the standing leg and engage your core. Slowly bend your standing leg, lowering your body as far as your strength and flexibility allow. Keep your extended leg straight and off the ground throughout the movement. Keep your weight on your heels and knees tracking over your toes. Engage your core to maintain a neutral spine.
  • Rising Phase:Press through the heel of your standing leg to return to the starting position. Maintain control and balance as you rise.
  • Repeat: Complete the desired number of repetitions on one leg, then switch to the other.

Sprint Intervals

Focus Full Body
Type Cardio
Difficulty Normal

How to Perform Sprint Intervals:

  • Warm-Up: Begin with a 5–10 minute dynamic warm-up to prepare your muscles and joints. Include light jogging, high knees, or dynamic stretches.
  • Sprint Phase:Run at maximum or near-maximum effort for a short duration or distance (e.g., 15–60 seconds or 50–200 meters). Focus on explosive speed, proper running form, and powerful strides.
  • Recovery Phase:Slow down to a walk or light jog to allow your heart rate to recover. This phase typically lasts 1–2 times the sprint duration (e.g., 30–120 seconds)..
  • Repeat: Alternate between sprints and recovery for a set number of rounds (e.g., 5–10 intervals) or a total time (e.g., 15–30 minutes).
  • Cool Down: End with 5–10 minutes of light jogging or walking, followed by static stretches to reduce muscle tension.
